# Urban Romance Story Seeds

Use this reference for urban romance, dog-blood melodrama, short-drama hooks, and emotionally charged relationship seeds.

## Seed Checklist

A strong seed usually has:

- a protagonist with a visible wound or desire
- a relationship that cannot be emotionally neutral
- a public-facing conflict and a private secret
- one image-like opening moment
- one twist that changes who has power
- one emotional promise: revenge, regret, forbidden pull, second chance, dignity, healing, or sharp sweetness

## Useful Relationship Engines

- ex-spouses forced into a new power balance
- older woman and younger man with social pressure
- single parent romance with protection, suspicion, or misunderstanding
- old classmates meeting after status reversal
- fake relationship becoming emotionally real
- rival families or rival business roles, fictionalized and non-confidential
- mistaken identity that creates intimacy before truth
- white moonlight, stand-in, or replacement lover reversed

## Short-Drama Hooks

- "The person I least wanted to see became my only witness."
- "He thought I needed saving; then he found out I owned the room."
- "I married the wrong brother, but the right one remembered everything."
- "My ex brought his new lover to humiliate me; my date called him by his full name."
- "The child asked one question, and every adult at the table went silent."

## Three-Direction Method

When the user has only one raw idea, generate three versions:

1. sweet-painful: emotional restraint, regret, second chance
2. sharp-revenge: status reversal, public face slap, dignity restored
3. forbidden-pull: morally tense attraction, secrets, near-misses

Ask the user which one makes them want to click "next episode."

## Avoid

- generic CEO cruelty with no emotional specificity
- a passive heroine who only suffers
- twists that rely on medical shock, pregnancy, or amnesia unless the user asks for extreme melodrama
- dumping a full outline before the user has chosen the emotional direction
- judging the user's taste; treat melodrama as a valid genre engine
